## Motivation

Before presenting the structural model, the proposal needs a section showing suggestive, model-free evidence that monopsony distorts hiring and promotion by gender. This section presents the descriptive plots from [Issue #2](https://github.com/sofiavaldiviac/BUK_sofia/issues/2) as reduced form facts that the model must rationalize.

## Specific goal

Write a 2–3 page reduced form evidence section covering:
1. **Women have fewer outside options** — job-to-job transition rates, spell durations, and separation rates by gender (Issue #2, Section 1)
2. **Market concentration varies** — HHI distribution across occupation × region cells; applicants-per-vacancy as a tightness measure (Issue #2, Section 2)
3. **Screening at hiring covaries with concentration** — application-to-hire conversion rates by gender, and how the gender gap changes across HHI terciles (Issue #2, Section 3)
4. **Promotion gaps widen with market power** — promotion rates and time-to-first-promotion by gender × HHI tercile (Issue #2, Section 4)
5. **Survey evidence on mechanisms** — pay equity perceptions, promotion satisfaction, and valued job attributes by gender (Issue #2, Section 5)
6. **Summary of stylized facts** — numbered list of 4–6 key facts that the model should match

## Success criteria

- [ ] Each fact is presented with a clear figure or table reference (from Issue #2 plots)
- [ ] Facts are organized to build the narrative: outside options → concentration → hiring → promotion
- [ ] No structural model language — this section is purely descriptive
- [ ] Explicit statement of what the facts suggest but cannot prove (motivates the model)
- [ ] Stylized facts are numbered and clearly stated for later reference in the model section
