## Motivation

The Work in Progress survey provides subjective measures (satisfaction, perceptions of equity, burnout, retention intentions) that complement the administrative BUK records. The proposal needs to describe what the survey offers and how it will be used, while being transparent about its limitations (open survey, not linkable to platform data).

## Specific goal

Write a 1–1.5 page subsection covering:
1. **Survey overview** — 6,789 respondents across Chile, Colombia, Mexico, Peru (2026)
2. **Design and limitations** — open survey (anyone can respond), cannot be linked to BUK platform data
3. **Topics covered** — demographics, salary, AI adoption, job satisfaction, burnout, gender equity perceptions, D&I policies, benefits, retention intentions
4. **Role in the paper** — subjective complement to administrative records; can provide suggestive evidence on mechanisms (e.g., do women in concentrated markets report lower satisfaction or fewer promotion opportunities?)
5. **Country breakdown** — emphasize Chile subsample for this paper, note cross-country comparisons as extension

## Success criteria

- [ ] Survey design and scope clearly described
- [ ] Limitations stated upfront (not linkable, open survey, selection concerns)
- [ ] Specific survey variables identified that map to the paper's mechanisms
- [ ] Clear distinction between what admin data vs. survey data can contribute
